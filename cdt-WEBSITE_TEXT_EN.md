## Demos

- **Section label:** Demos
- **Title:** See the platform in action.
- **Subtitle:** Short walk-throughs of the platform in action — from the general overview to focused tools built for specific use cases.

**Platform overview**
A guided tour of the digital twin workspace — bringing BIM, GIS, and open data together in a single browser session, with the tools institutions actually use day-to-day.

**Platform viewers**
Map, BIM, and point-cloud viewers backed by site, building, and file databases — searchable through buildingSMART Data Dictionary properties. IFC, GeoJSON, point clouds, documents, and media live alongside each building, and the platform is extensible via custom plugins.

**BIM + GIS integration**
Building-scale BIM and infrastructure-scale GIS data load together in a single browser environment, with precise geolocation and support for multiple coordinate systems. Multi-gigabyte portfolios resolve in under two seconds per model, and users can drop geolocated comments and models onto the map to collaborate.

**Open data federation**
Federates dozens of open government datasets pulled through standardized APIs with no manual preparation, and users can connect their own open data sources from anywhere in the world. Layer neighbourhoods, transit, infrastructure, and more alongside your buildings, then tune colour scales and visualization quality for analysis.

**openBIM viewer**
Browser-native IFC processing that preserves the full schema — every attribute, material, constraint, and custom property set. Add DXF, GLTF, FBX, OBJ, or point-cloud overlays; section, measure, and comment on the geometry; track issues with BCF; validate models against IDS exchange requirements.

**IoT sensors on the map and BIM**
Add live IoT sensors directly onto the map and into the BIM model. Stream readings into the digital twin to connect physical infrastructure with its digital counterpart.

**Visualizing wildfires**
An interactive map built with a wildfire research group to monitor fire conditions across Canada — combining open datasets from the Canadian Interagency Forest Fire Centre (CIFFC) with photos and videos crowdsourced from users in near-real time.

## FAQ

> Note: this section is currently commented out on the live Resources page, but the text exists in the source.

**Where is our data hosted, and can we choose the region?**
With managed hosting, the platform runs on sovereign infrastructure in the region you choose, so your data stays within the jurisdiction you require. If you self-host, your data never leaves your own infrastructure.

**Who can access our data?**
Only the people you authorize. We operate on least-privilege access, and on self-managed deployments no one outside your organization has access at all. We never sell or share your data.

**Is the platform really open source?**
Yes. The core is released under the AGPL-3.0 licence, so you can read, audit, self-host, and modify the source. There is no proprietary black box.

**How do we avoid vendor lock-in?**
Because the platform is built on open standards (IFC, GeoJSON, buildingSMART Data Dictionary) and open source, you can export your data and self-host the core at any time. Your investment is portable.

**Is our data encrypted?**
Yes. Data is encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest on managed deployments. Self-hosted deployments inherit your own infrastructure's encryption and network controls.

**Can we self-host on our own infrastructure?**
Absolutely. The AGPL-3.0 core can be deployed entirely on-premise or in your own cloud, giving you complete control over data, network, and integrations.

**How is the project governed?**
Collab Digital Twins is a board-governed Canadian non-profit. We operate on cost-recovery pricing with no profit margin, and any surplus is reinvested in the platform and community.

**How can we run a security review or report a vulnerability?**
We welcome security reviews. Because the source is open, your team can audit it directly, and you can reach us at info@collabdt.org to coordinate a review or report a vulnerability.
